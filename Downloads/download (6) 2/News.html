The Soviet Union was supposed to be a true democracy society but it was no less repressive than the czarist autocracy that was before. It was ruled by a single party–the Communist Party–that demanded the allegiance of every Russian citizen. After 1924, when Joseph Stalin the dictator came to power, the state exercised totalitarian control over the economy, administering all industrial activity and establishing collective farms. It also controlled every aspect of political and social life. People who argued against Stalin’s policies were arrested and sent to labor camps or executed.
